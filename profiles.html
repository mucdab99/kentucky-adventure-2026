Brian Family Trail Roster

Different hiking styles. Different trail fails. Same goal: unforgettable Kentucky memories.

🥾

Annalise

The Trail Curmudgeon
Age: 16
Hiking Style

A true trail curmudgeon at heart, although she has started finding her inner hiker of late. Annalise will quickly declare, “this view is good enough” when the going gets tough. On her latest outing in Hawaii, she posted her best efforts yet and is poised to earn her Brian Family merit badge for Hiking Aptitude.

Trail Fails

Where do we start? Lake Tahoe, where she tripped and reopened a gaping knee wound from a neighbor’s log incident a month prior. Or the time she went full rebellion on a hike in Cook Forest, PA and had to be “rescued” by a duo of elderly ladies.

🧘

Mom

The Trail Whisperer
Age: 48 (or 50 according to her)
Hiking Style

Trail whisperer at heart. Mom is in it for finding inner peace and rebalancing her soul. She is the yin to Dad’s yang. Physical exertion and basking in the abundance of nature are what drive Mom.

Trail Fails

Anyone else here lost a critical piece of gear on a hike? Mom lost a shoe that was tied off to her backpack approximately 0.3 miles into a 7-mile hike up Mt. LeConte for our 20th anniversary trip. Never fear though — her Knight in Shining Armor doubled back and located the missing shoe.

⚽

Joe

The Reliable Hiking Partner
Age: 13
Hiking Style

Indoctrinated to the ways of the trail from an early age, Joe has been a reliable hiking partner. As he matures, the goal is to make sure he doesn’t despise hiking for the rest of his life because of parent-forced “death marches.”

Trail Fails

Joe decided it would be a great idea to contract Lyme disease before our Hawaii trip last year. A 1,500-foot elevation gain hike? Joe was gassed at 15 feet of elevation gain and also decided to barf multiple times on the way up and down Mt. Haleakalā.

🏃

Emmy

The Trail Blazer
Age: 18
Hiking Style

A trailblazer at heart. Sets a quick tempo focused on achieving the objective.

Trail Fails

Summited Mt. LeConte in Tennessee, only to succumb to hubris and promptly fall flat on her backside trying to balance-beam walk a mossy log. Classic.

🧭

Dad

The Relentless Pacemaker
Age: 48
Hiking Style

Dad is incapable of seeing the forest for the trees. A relentless pacemaker on the trail, he will ditch his entire family in the name of “keeping up the pace.” Self-imposed deadlines, however irrational, are his bread and butter.

Trail Fails

Expert in orienteering, Dad is not. He once got his entire family lost in the Cleveland Metroparks. Then there was the time he successfully got himself and Mom lost in Rocky Mountain National Park amid trails obscured by 6 feet of snowpack — almost triggering the awkward “you guys are going to live with the Krems!” outcome as Mom and Dad perished in the snow.

Adventure is better when we do it together.

And apparently better when at least one person is keeping pace, one person is finding inner peace, and everyone is trying not to lose shoes, reopen knee wounds, or barf on volcanoes.
